About

About page coming soon.

Sarah Gerth is an educator, curator, and knowledge designer working at the intersection of art, place, and learning in New York City. She builds programs, exhibitions, and curricula — almost always in collaboration — that take seriously the idea that the city itself is a site of inquiry.

Her work spans museum education, arts-based curriculum design, faculty development, and public scholarship. She holds a doctorate from Teachers College, Columbia University, where her dissertation examined place, affect, and artful practices for curriculum theory.

She is a co-founder of Shapes of Knowledge, a studio for creative engagements with knowledge, and a faculty member at City Seminary of New York.
